Executive Summary

Anthropic is the fastest-growing AI company in history, scaling from $1B to $19B in annualized revenue in just 15 months. Founded in 2021 by former OpenAI leadership — Dario Amodei (CEO) and Daniela Amodei (President) — the company has positioned Claude as the enterprise AI platform of choice. Eight of the Fortune 10 are customers, and 70% of first-time enterprise AI buyers choose Anthropic over OpenAI. Claude Code alone has reached $2.5B ARR within nine months of launch. The company completed a $30B Series G at a $380B valuation in February 2026, making it one of the most valuable private companies globally.

However, the company faces significant legal headwinds: a $1.5B copyright settlement (Bartz v. Anthropic, the largest AI copyright case ever), a $3B music publisher lawsuit from Universal/Concord/BMG, and an unprecedented Pentagon “supply chain risk” designation after Anthropic refused to remove safety guardrails for lethal autonomous warfare applications. Two security incidents in a single week in March 2026 — a Mythos model leak via unsecured CMS and a Claude Code source code leak via npm packaging error — raise operational security concerns despite SOC 2 compliance.

The safety mission is deeply embedded in company culture (Glassdoor 4.4/5, 93% CEO approval), but the recent RSP v3.0 policy change — which removed the pledge to pause training if safety cannot keep up — has drawn criticism from the AI safety community, Time, and CNN. Overall risk posture is CAUTION (amber): extraordinary financial strength and market momentum, offset by material legal exposure, government conflict, and emerging operational security gaps.
