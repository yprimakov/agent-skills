Reduce Technology Overweight — Trim QQQ from 20% to 14–15%
Sell $9,000–$10,800 of QQQ (5–6% of portfolio). The 20% allocation to QQQ represents the single largest risk concentration in a high-rate, inflationary environment. QQQ's 28x forward P/E is acutely sensitive to rate repricing. Reallocate proceeds into: Energy ETF (XLE) +3–4%, Financials (XLF) +2–3%, or TIPS (TIP) +2–3%. This single action reduces portfolio rate sensitivity by an estimated 15% and improves inflation alignment.

Increase Inflation Hedge Allocation — GLD 5% to 7–8%, Add TIPS
Current 5% inflation protection ($9,000 GLD) is dangerously insufficient for the primary risk scenario. Target: increase inflation-protective assets to 10–12% of portfolio ($18,000–$21,600). Action: (a) Add $3,600–$5,400 to GLD, bringing allocation to 7–8%. (b) Initiate TIP (iShares TIPS Bond ETF) position at 3% ($5,400) from proceeds of TLT reduction. This doubles inflation hedge coverage from 5% to 10–11% at minimal cost to expected returns in base/bull scenarios (+0.3% drag) while dramatically reducing bear case loss from −12.2% to ~−8%.
