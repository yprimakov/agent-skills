Why This Portfolio Is Tax-Efficient

  • ETFs vs. Mutual Funds: ETFs use in-kind redemption — over 5 years, only 17% of active ETFs paid out capital gains vs. 76% of active mutual funds.
  • Low Turnover Indexes: VTI, VXUS, and BND track passive indexes with minimal rebalancing, resulting in very few taxable events.
  • Growth Tilt Reduces Dividends: QQQ and VGT hold growth-oriented companies that reinvest earnings rather than pay dividends — reducing ordinary income distributions.
  • Qualified Dividends: VTI and VXUS dividends are largely qualified, taxed at 0–20% LTCG rate rather than 22–24% ordinary income rate.

Tax-Loss Harvesting Strategy

  • Monitor Positions: Review monthly for unrealized losses exceeding $500. Tax-loss harvest before year-end to offset any capital gains.
  • Wash-Sale Substitutes: If harvesting VTI loss, temporarily hold SCHB or ITOT (different index) for 31 days to avoid wash-sale rule.
  • Harvest VXUS vs. IEFA: Use iShares Core MSCI EAFE (IEFA, 0.07% ER) as a VXUS substitute during loss harvesting windows.
  • Annual Savings Estimate: At $120k income (22% bracket), harvesting $3,000/year in losses saves ~$660/year in federal taxes.

Bond Placement Warning

  • BND Caveat: With a 10-yr yield at 4.26% and a 22–24% marginal tax bracket, BND's interest income is taxed as ordinary income — reducing after-tax yield to ~3.2–3.3%.
  • Alternative Consideration: If you have a 401(k) or IRA available, hold BND there and replace with more VTI/VXUS in this taxable account.
  • Muni Bond Alternative: Tax-exempt municipal bond ETFs (MUB, VTEB) may be more efficient at your income level if bond allocation grows.
  • SGOV Advantage: Treasury income from SGOV is exempt from state income taxes — a meaningful benefit in higher-tax states.

Long-Term Holding Strategy

  • Hold >1 Year: Always hold positions at least 12 months to qualify for long-term capital gains rates (0%, 15%, or 20% at your income level — likely 15%).
  • Step-Up in Basis: ETFs held long-term benefit from potential step-up in cost basis at death — a significant estate planning advantage.
  • Avoid Frequent Rebalancing: Rebalance primarily by directing new contributions to underweight positions — minimizes taxable events.
  • Dividend Reinvestment: Consider manually reinvesting dividends into the most underweight position rather than using DRIP — provides free rebalancing without selling.
