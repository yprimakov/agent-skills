Rebalancing Policy
  • Annual Rebalancing: Full portfolio review and rebalance in January each year, aligned with new fiscal year planning.
  • 5% Drift Trigger: Any asset class drifting more than ±5% from target triggers an interim rebalance regardless of calendar date.
  • Tax-Loss Harvesting Window: December review for realized losses in volatile ETFs (QQQ, IEMG, GLD). Harvest losses to offset gains; reinvest in correlated positions to maintain exposure.
  • Contribution Rebalancing: Monthly $2,000 DCA contributions are allocated first to underweight positions to reduce frequency of full rebalances.
  • Threshold Monitoring: Quarterly review of allocations. If no asset has drifted beyond 5%, no action is taken — minimize transaction friction.
Rebalancing Calendar

January: Full annual rebalance + forward allocation review.

April / July / October: Quarterly drift check — rebalance only if 5% trigger is breached.

December: Tax-loss harvest window — evaluate QQQ, IEMG, GLD for unrealized losses. Swap candidates: QQQM for QQQ; SCHE for IEMG; IAUM for GLD (30-day wash-sale avoidance).

Estimated Annual Tax Drag

For this taxable brokerage portfolio, the estimated annual tax drag is approximately 0.30% of portfolio value, assuming a 24% marginal federal rate and 80% qualified dividend treatment on equity ETFs. This compares favorably to actively managed funds (typical drag: 1.0–2.0%).

Tax-Loss Harvesting Potential

The satellite sleeve (QQQ, IEMG, GLD, VNQ) offers natural loss-harvesting opportunities during volatility spikes. Estimated annual tax alpha from harvesting: 0.15–0.40%, net of wash-sale management costs. Reinvest proceeds within 30 days into correlated alternatives to maintain factor exposure.
