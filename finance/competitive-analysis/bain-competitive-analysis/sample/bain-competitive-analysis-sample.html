Amazon / AWS (AMZN) — Primary Recommendation

Strengths

  • Dominant 30% global cloud market share with 200+ services — widest ecosystem breadth
  • $39.8B operating income from AWS alone (37% margin) — unmatched cash generation
  • $200B 2026 capex commitment — largest absolute AI infrastructure investment
  • Custom silicon (Trainium / Inferentia) reducing AI compute costs by 30–40% vs. GPU alternatives
  • $88.5B R&D spend (#1 globally) sustaining technical lead across AWS + Alexa + Robotics
  • Amazon Prime, Ads, and Retail provide a diversified revenue floor if cloud slows
  • AWS Marketplace — largest commercial cloud marketplace, creating partner ecosystem stickiness

Weaknesses

  • AWS market share gradually compressing from 32% (Q4 2024) to 30% (Q2 2025)
  • Lagging AI model layer vs. Microsoft (OpenAI exclusivity) and Google (DeepMind/Gemini)
  • $200B capex in one year creates significant FCF pressure and balance sheet risk
  • Retail segment drag on consolidated margins — cloud profit subsidizes lower-margin businesses
  • Enterprise AI tooling (Bedrock) still catching up to Azure's Copilot commercial deployments
  • US regulatory antitrust scrutiny of Prime and AWS bundling practices increasing

Opportunities

  • AI inference demand exploding — AWS positioned as the dominant training+inference compute layer
  • Sovereign cloud expansion: new regions in Asia, Middle East, Europe opening new regulated workloads
  • Amazon Bedrock multi-model marketplace (Claude, Llama, Titan) capturing enterprise AI spend
  • Healthcare, government, and manufacturing verticals still early in cloud migration
  • AWS supply-chain robotics + logistics tech commercialization opportunity (Project Kuiper satellite broadband)
  • Advertising business ($50B+) generating incremental margin uplift with limited incremental capital

Threats

  • Capex ROI gap: $25B AI revenue vs. ~$450B industry AI capex — bubble risk if adoption disappoints
  • Google Cloud gaining share (+1 ppt in 2025) with aggressive pricing and Gemini differentiation
  • Microsoft's OpenAI exclusivity creates a differentiated AI moat Amazon cannot easily replicate
  • Tariff and geopolitical risk to global supply chain for servers and semiconductors
  • Oracle, Snowflake, and neo-cloud providers winning niche AI workloads away from AWS
  • Rising debt financing needed: hyperscalers raised $108B in debt in 2025 alone
