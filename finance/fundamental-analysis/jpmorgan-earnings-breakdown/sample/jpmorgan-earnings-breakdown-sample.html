Key Metrics Wall Street Is Watching
  • Data Center Revenue vs. $38.9B Consensus — The single most watched line item. Any print above $40B would be materially positive; below $36B would trigger a significant sell-off. Hyperscaler spending plans through H1 2026 are the primary driver.
  • Blackwell GPU Shipment Volumes & Ramp Velocity — Wall Street wants confirmation of an accelerating Blackwell ramp. Management commentary on GB200 NVL72 rack-scale system availability is the key qualitative signal this quarter.
  • China Revenue Disclosure — Following BIS export restrictions in October 2023 and subsequent rule tightening, China revenue has been in structural decline. Any update on H20 chip demand or workaround solutions for the Chinese market will move the stock.
  • Gross Margin Trajectory — Blackwell systems carry higher initial COGS due to new packaging and supply chain complexity. Consensus expects 74.8%; a print above 75% would signal the ramp cost headwinds are clearing faster than expected.
  • Q2 FY2026 Revenue Guidance — Given NVDA's history of conservative guidance followed by large beats, management's Q2 guide will be dissected for any softening in demand language. The market is pricing ~$46-47B for Q2.
  • Hyperscaler Capex Commentary — Management's characterization of customer backlog, lead times, and forward purchasing commitments from Microsoft, Google, Amazon, and Meta will be scrutinized for any demand softening signals.
  • Networking Revenue (Spectrum-X) — NVDA's Ethernet networking business is emerging as a significant incremental growth driver. Any color on attach rates and competitive dynamics vs. Broadcom will be closely watched.

J.P. Morgan Recommendation

BUY BEFORE EARNINGS — Initiating at OVERWEIGHT, $165 Price Target

Thesis We initiate coverage of NVIDIA (NVDA) with an Overweight rating and a 12-month price target of $165, implying 44.5% upside from the current price of $114.20. Our thesis is grounded in three pillars: (1) the structural underpenetration of AI infrastructure relative to secular demand, (2) NVIDIA's unassailable competitive moat via the CUDA ecosystem, and (3) an accelerating Blackwell ramp that consensus estimates are too conservative on.

Earnings Setup Heading into the Q1 FY2026 print in late May, the risk/reward is favorable. NVDA trades at $114, having corrected 31% from its November 2024 high of $165 — driven by export control uncertainty and broader semiconductor multiple compression. At current levels, the stock prices in roughly zero Blackwell upside on our estimates, creating an asymmetric setup with limited downside relative to significant potential upside if Blackwell demand tracks our conviction.

Options Strategy We recommend the May 2026 $120/$130 call spread at an approximately $3.20 debit (indicative). The structure offers ~$6.80 of maximum profit (212% return on premium) if NVDA settles above $130 on expiry — consistent with a +14% move from spot, which is below the historical average post-earnings move of +11.2% for positive prints. Maximum loss is the $3.20 debit. Risk/reward is approximately 2.1:1. For larger position sizes, consider laddering into the $110/$120 call spread as well.

Risk/Reward Bull case to $175–190 (+53–67%) vs. bear case to $95–100 (−17–21%). On a probability-weighted basis (65% bull / 35% bear, at midpoints), our expected value suggests approximately $152–155 fair value, comfortably above current levels. Key risks to our thesis include incremental export restrictions, hyperscaler demand deceleration, and competitive GPU alternatives gaining enterprise traction.
